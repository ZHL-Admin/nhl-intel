# Dependency probe — findings (behavioral-dependency angle on fit)

Third angle on "fit" after two output-based nulls (F17 pair residual; F18 unit over-performance):
**individual behavior conditioned on teammates.** The outcome is a property of ONE player, which
sidesteps the identifiability walls. Frozen/already-ingested-read corpus, primary window 2015-16→
2025-26, deployment (and, Round 2, opponent) controlled throughout. Held pending the owner's F-number
ruling; recorded here so the tested results are preserved regardless of what is built.

## The map, across three rounds

**What IS partner-conditioned (real):**
- **The shoot-vs-defer decision.** A player's *share* of his pair's shot attempts genuinely moves by
  partner (Link A: partner-deviation split-half reliability **0.352**, beats placebo p=0.002, absolute
  across-partner SD ≈ 0.10 share points, **fully survives** the OZ+score+opponent deployment control,
  strengthens with more shared minutes). With one partner a player takes ~55% of the pair's shots,
  with another ~45%. This is the ONLY real partner tendency among all axes tested.
- His shot **rate** does not move — volume is his own; what changes is whether he **shoots or defers**.

**What is the PLAYER'S OWN, not partner-driven (tested nulls — Round 2, Link Q):**
- **Chance quality** (xG per unblocked attempt), **on-goal rate**, blocked/missed share — player-
  constant (reliability ~0, never beat placebo). *We tested whether chance quality is partner-driven,
  and it is not.*
- **Shot location** (inner-slot share, mean distance) — player-constant. A player brings his own shot
  map; the teammate doesn't move it.
- **Finishing** (shooting %) — player-constant (and denominator-thin; noise).
- **Penalty** tendencies (taken/drawn) — player-constant.
- None of these were "deployment in disguise" — they never moved raw, so the opponent control was not
  what killed them. (Consistent with role-fit F18: the shot axes were the most *player-carried*.)

**Not recoverable (honest drops, not inferred — the Round-1 feeding lesson):**
- **Icing** (Q5): the frozen Atlas events carry no stoppage `reason`, and icing is team-only upstream
  — not individually attributable. Dropped.
- **Zone-exit / breakout** (Q6): no exit/entry/carry event type exists; recovery needs timing-inference.
  Dropped.
- **Feeding** (Round 1): a shot-adjacent timing shadow only, too weak to weight; true passing is
  off-puck/unrecorded (Tier iii).

**But dependence is not a stable TRAIT you can build on (Link B):**
- Collapsing shot-share swing-across-partners into a per-player dependence score: real but weak and it
  doesn't travel — split-half **0.15** (bar 0.40), same-team YoY **0.20** (bar 0.30), **across-team
  retention 0.28**. Measured too noisily (3–6 partners/season) to be a predictive feature. Link C
  (post-departure prediction) not justified.
- *Who is context-dependent* (descriptive): offensive/sheltered/high-usage players adjust who-shoots
  by partner (F:top-PP-sheltered-offense +0.19 z); defensive/checking players do not
  (F:bottom-EV-tough-defense −0.20 z).

## Standing conclusion
Fit has now been tested four ways and clears no usability bar as a *predictive* quantity: pair residual
(F17), unit over-performance (F18), and behavioral dependence — real pairwise, but not a stable
individual trait (this probe). The **durable results are descriptive**: shooting deference is a real
pairwise behavior; chance quality/location/finishing are player constants; offensive players are the
context-dependent ones. Nothing promoted or published. F-number held for the program ledger until the
owner rules.
