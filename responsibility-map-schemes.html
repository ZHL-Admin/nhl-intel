Drag the puck around the defensive zone and watch the shape of each defender's responsibility area change. Switch the coverage scheme to see how the same five positions carve up the ice differently. These are idealized, league-general templates, not any one team's system and not a data claim.

Each region is the area of ice closest to that defender's responsibility center, interpolated over eight puck anchors. With puck pressure on, a defender steps out of his resting center toward the puck when it enters his area, hard in man and swarm, barely at all in zone, and the dashed marker shows the resting spot he left. Toggle it off for the plain responsibility map.
